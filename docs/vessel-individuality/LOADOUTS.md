## Notes

- **LA Class Flight I** had no VLS; all strike weapons were tube-launched including TLAM and TASM. SUBROC and TASM were retired in the early 1990s.
- **LA Class Flight II** (SSN 719–750) added 12 VLS tubes for Tomahawk. **Flight III / 688i** (SSN 751–773) retained VLS and added improved sensors and under-ice capability.
- **Seawolf Class** has the largest tube count (8) and largest magazine (50) of any US attack submarine, and the widest tubes (26.5") allowing a greater variety of payloads. No VLS was ever fitted.
- **Swiftsure Class** Tomahawk capability was not fleet-wide — most notably HMS Splendid and HMS Spartan were upgraded. Magazine was the smallest of the RN SSN classes at 20 weapons.
- **Trafalgar Class** expanded the magazine to 30 and Tomahawk was more broadly integrated across the class. Sub-Harpoon was retired around 2004 and not replaced, leaving a gap in the dedicated anti-ship missile role.
- **Type 209** is the only conventional (diesel-electric) submarine in this comparison. Magazine capacity of 14 is notably smaller than nuclear boats. Mine capacity can be significantly increased with external containers on some variants.
